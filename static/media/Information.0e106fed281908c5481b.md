### HRA Calculation method
The exemption on HRA is calculated as per 2A of the Income Tax Rules. As per Rule 2A, the least of the following is exempted from salary under Section 10(13A) and does not form part of the taxable income. 
* Actual HRA received from the employer
* For those living in metro cities: 50% of (Basic salary + Dearness allowance)
* For those living in non-metro cities: 40% of (Basic salary + Dearness allowance)
* Actual rent paid minus 10% of (Basic salary + Dearness allowance)

### Metro Cities for HRA

For the purpose of determining in a metro city, metro cities are Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, and Delhi. The city is not a metropolis in all other cases.

Why does it matter? HRA Calculation differs on whether you reside in a metro city. See [HRA Calculation method](#hra-calculation-method) section

### Professional Tax
Professional tax is a direct tax imposed by state governments on individuals who earn an income through employment or practicing a profession. It is deducted by employers from salaries and paid to the state government. Not all states levy professional tax, and the rates and regulations may change.

Find out the Professional Tax for your state [here](https://www.paisabazaar.com/tax/professional-tax/).

### Investment Categories

##### Section 80 C
Section 80C allows deductions on investments and expenses. It includes options like EPF, PPF, life insurance premiums, home loan principal, tuition fees, NPS, and more. The maximum deduction limit is ₹1.5 lakh per year.

##### Section 80 CCD(1B) NPS Investment
Section 80CCD(1B) is a provision that offers additional deductions for contributions made towards the National Pension Scheme (NPS). Taxpayers can claim deductions up to ₹50,000 over and above the limit of Section 80C. It is an effective way to save on taxes while planning for retirement.

##### Section 80 CCD(2) Employer NPS
Section 80CCD(2) allows employees to claim additional deductions on contributions made by their employers towards the National Pension Scheme (NPS). The deduction can be up to 10% of the employee's salary, including basic pay and dearness allowance. This deduction is in addition to the deductions available under Section 80C and 80CCD(1). It provides an extra tax benefit for individuals who have an employer-sponsored NPS account.

##### Section 80 D 
Section 80D provides deductions for expenses incurred on health insurance premiums and medical expenditures. Taxpayers can claim deductions for premiums paid for health insurance policies covering themselves, their spouse, children, and parents. The deduction limit varies based on the age of the insured individuals and can range from ₹25,000 to ₹1,00,000. 

##### Section 24 (B) Home Loan Interest
Under Section 24(b), individuals can claim deductions on the interest paid on a home loan for a self-occupied or let-out property. The maximum deduction allowed is ₹2 lakh per year for a self-occupied property, while there is no upper limit for a let-out property.

##### Section 80 EE Home Loan Interest 
Section 80EE of the Indian Income Tax Act provides additional deductions on home loan interest for first-time homebuyers. This section allows individuals to claim deductions of up to ₹50,000 per year on the interest paid on home loans. The loan must be sanctioned between April 1, 2016, and March 31, 2017, and the value of the property should not exceed ₹50 lakh. Additionally, the taxpayer should not own any other residential property at the time of loan sanction

Read more in detail [here (www.incometax.gov.in.)](https://www.incometax.gov.in/iec/foportal/help/individual/return-applicable-0/#taxdeductions).
